# Retail-Consumer-Behaviour-Analysis-Tableau-Data-Visualisation
Retail Consumer Behaviour Analysis — Tableau Data Visualisation
Tool: Tableau | Course: BUSN5003 | University: University of Western Australia
What this project does
Analyses a retail sales dataset to uncover insights around product profitability, shipping method seasonality, and consumer shopping preferences across age groups. Three key business insights are visualised and presented using Tableau dashboards.
Key insights explored
1. Profit vs product ratings across categories
Electronics generated the highest profit (~1.09M) with excellent ratings, while Home Décor showed profitability but suffered from poor customer ratings. Grocery maintained balanced ratings despite lower profits.
2. Seasonal profitability of shipping methods
Same-day and express shipping surged in profitability during November–December holiday season, showing customers willingness to pay a premium for faster delivery. Standard shipping remained consistently lower throughout the year.
3. Shopping preferences across age groups
Electronics dominated spending across all age groups (18–70). Younger consumers (18–30) showed notable spending on groceries (0.60M) and books (0.55M), revealing targeted campaign opportunities.
Data processing steps

Checked and confirmed no duplicate records
Converted data types: age/units/ratings to integer, price/revenue/profit to 3 decimal numerical
Filtered null values within Tableau
Renamed columns for clarity (e.g. Product Category → Category)
Created two calculated fields: age group segmentation (18–30, 31–40, 41–50, 51–70) and numerical ratings converted to descriptive labels (excellent, very good, good, fair, poor)

Visualisation choices

Bar charts for profit by category and rating comparisons
Line chart for monthly shipping profit trends with average reference line and holiday season highlight (Nov–Dec)
Decluttering techniques: removed gridlines, adjusted axis intervals, added descriptive titles and reference lines
